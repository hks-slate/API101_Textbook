Consumer & Producer Surplus

Review welfare analysis and the policy tradeoffs created by price controls.

Scope of Practice Quiz

This checkpoint focuses on the core Module 5 skills. Use it to test whether you can calculate surplus, distinguish surplus transfers from deadweight loss, and interpret welfare effects of policy.

Consumer surplus measures the value buyers receive above what they pay. Producer surplus measures the value sellers receive above the minimum they would accept. Social surplus is the sum of the two.

Policies such as price floors can transfer surplus across groups, but they can also reduce total surplus by preventing mutually beneficial transactions. That lost value is deadweight loss.
