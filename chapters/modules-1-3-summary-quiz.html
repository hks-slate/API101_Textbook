Demand, Supply, and Equilibrium

Review the core market model before moving into curve shifters.

Scope of Practice Quiz

Modules 1-3 build the basic supply-and-demand model. Demand describes how the quantity that consumers buy responds to price changes; supply describes how the quantity that producers sell responds to price changes; equilibrium is the price such that the quantity demanded equates the quantity supplied.

The CAP example is a policy application of the supply-and-demand model. CAP's price above the equilibrium price reduces quantity demanded, increases quantity supplied, and creates a surplus that government must absorb and pay for.

Practice Quiz

This practice quiz checks the core skills from Modules 1-3: reading demand and supply equations, solving for equilibrium, identifying surplus and shortage, and applying the same logic to the CAP price-support example. You can work through the questions in any order and receive feedback after each response.

Keep paper and a pen or pencil nearby so you can sketch graphs and work through calculations as you answer.
