API-101 Microeconomics Modules

Credits and Acknowledgments

This interactive textbook reflects a collaborative effort to make core microeconomic concepts more accessible, visual, and practice-oriented for API-101 students.

Credits

The current site brings together faculty-authored material, interactive development, learning design, and generative AI support.

Teaching Fellow Author and Developer

Jacob Jameson authored and developed the interactive textbook experience. His work included drafting and adapting instructional content, designing the interactive visualizations, building the graphing tools and practice activities, developing the quiz and self-check systems, and translating faculty-authored material into a web-based learning sequence for API-101 students.

Faculty Authors

Pınar Doğan and Juan Saavedra shaped the academic foundation of this learning experience, including the core microeconomic concepts, policy framing, and instructional priorities for API-101.

Generative AI Consultant

Mae Klinger led the design of the course chatbot that accompanies these materials. The chatbot was developed to serve as a supportive tutor as students work through the interactive textbook, helping them reason through concepts, practice problems, and points of confusion. Juan Saavedra and Jacob Jameson also contributed to the generative AI components of the learning experience.

Digital Learning Designer

Maria Flanagan guided the organization, accessibility, and instructional design of the materials. Her work helped ensure that the content was coherent, navigable, student-centered, and ready for use in Canvas and the online textbook environment.

Acknowledgments

This learning experience builds on a first edition authored by Janina Matuszeski, Felix Owusu, and Pınar Doğan, with excerpts adapted from the Principles of Economics textbook from OpenStax.
